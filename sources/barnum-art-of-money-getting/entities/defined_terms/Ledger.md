Barnum prescribes keeping a ledger divided into "necessaries" and "luxuries" columns as a practical accounting method. Recording daily expenditure reveals spending patterns and serves as a "cure for extravagance." The ledger enables awareness of hidden luxuries, revealing that most people spend ten times more on luxuries than necessities. This method transforms abstract spending consciousness into concrete data that exposes the gap between perceived and actual expenditure.
