Barnum asserts that business success depends critically on selecting proper location matching demand. Even a skilled hotel keeper locating in a small village without railroad communication will fail due to location alone. Equally ruinous is opening where the market is already saturated. He cites his London penny showman friend who relocated to America and became worth sixty thousand dollars through proper location selection, demonstrating that location changes can transform business outcomes independent of skill.
