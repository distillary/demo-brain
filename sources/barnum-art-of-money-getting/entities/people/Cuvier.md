French naturalist Cuvier exemplifies Barnum's principle that [[Deep knowledge prevents deception]]. Barnum praises Cuvier for knowing natural history so thoroughly that from a single bone he could deduce the animal's complete form. When students tried deceiving him with a costumed person in a cow skin, Cuvier immediately recognized the deception through analyzing anatomical properties. Cuvier demonstrates how thorough business knowledge enables recognition of fraudulent details.
