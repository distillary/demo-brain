Barnum argues that every customer interaction either compounds or erodes business value. [[Politeness retains customer relationships|Politeness and civility]] are the best [[Capital]] a merchant can invest, because customers return to kind treatment and flee from rudeness. The cost of a single lapse is disproportionate: [[Customer insult loses future revenue|insulting a paying customer]] loses not just that individual but everyone they influence. Beyond transactions, [[Charity produces business benefits|charitable giving]] creates a reputation that commands patronage even when pursued purely as business policy. The most effective form is [[Targeted charity achieves more good|selective assistance to self-helpers]], which produces the greatest benefit per unit of [[Charity]].
