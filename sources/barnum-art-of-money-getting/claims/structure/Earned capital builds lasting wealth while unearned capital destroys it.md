The process of earning money is itself the education that makes keeping money possible. [[Starting with borrowed capital prevents real wealth building|Borrowed capital]] fails because the borrower never learns what money costs in effort, while [[Self-earned capital teaches discipline and value|self-earned capital]] encodes discipline directly into the earner's habits. This explains why [[Poor boys rise more reliably than rich boys|poor boys outperform rich boys]] across generations: struggle teaches what comfort cannot. Conversely, [[Wealth without earned understanding corrupts character|unearned wealth corrupts character]] by removing the feedback loop between effort and reward. [[Easy access to credit encourages dangerous speculation|Easy credit]] accelerates this corruption by making risk feel costless.
