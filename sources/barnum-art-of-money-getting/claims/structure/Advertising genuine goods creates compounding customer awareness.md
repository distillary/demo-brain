Barnum treats advertising as a necessary bridge between a good product and its market. [[Quality products can succeed with advertising|A genuine article]] must be made known through persistent [[Advertising]], because the public cannot buy what it does not know exists. The mechanism is gradual: [[Repetition creates purchasing intention|seven exposures]] move a reader from unawareness through deliberation to purchase. But this compounding only works for honest goods[[Fraudulent goods lose customers permanently|advertising spurious articles]] creates initial sales followed by permanent denunciation, because the public is shrewder than merchants assume and will not return to an impostor.
