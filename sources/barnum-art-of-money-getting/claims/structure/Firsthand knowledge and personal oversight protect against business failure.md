No employee can match the owner's attention to detail, which is why [[Personal oversight exceeds delegation|the employer's eye outweighs a dozen employees' hands]]. This oversight is not mere supervision but deep understanding: [[Mistakes are teaching opportunities|mistakes become experience]] only when the owner observes them personally, and [[Deep knowledge prevents deception|thorough knowledge]] is the only reliable defense against fraud. The same principle applies at the strategic level[[Speculation outside expertise destroys wealth|venturing outside one's expertise]] eliminates the very knowledge advantage that created success. Barnum resolves the tension between paralysis and recklessness through [[Rothschild]]'s maxim: [[Caution and boldness both necessary|be cautious in planning and bold in execution]], combining deep knowledge with decisive action.
