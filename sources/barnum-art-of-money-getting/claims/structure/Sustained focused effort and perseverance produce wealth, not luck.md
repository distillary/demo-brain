Barnum identifies the core mechanism of wealth creation as concentrated, sustained effort applied to a single pursuit. [[Perseverance determines success|Perseverance]] separates those who reach the goal from those who quit just short of it. [[Wholesale effort trumps half measures|Doing business with full might]] is what distinguishes the prosperous from the merely competent. The enemy of this concentration is distraction: [[Constant business changing keeps one poor|jumping between ventures]] scatters energy, while [[Focused effort beats scattered powers|undivided attention]] generates the compounding improvements that create [[Wealth]]. Attributing outcomes to [[Luck does not exist|luck]] is the final evasionlike causes produce like effects, and proper methods cannot be permanently thwarted by [[Luck]].
