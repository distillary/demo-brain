Barnum's foundational principle is that [[Wealth]] begins with a single arithmetic rule: income must consistently exceed expenditure. But the real threat to this margin is not obvious extravagance; it is the subtle expansion of lifestyle driven by [[Social comparison drives wasteful spending|social comparison]], [[Prosperity requires self-regulation|unregulated prosperity]], and [[One purchase triggers cascading expenses|cascading purchases]] that each seem small in isolation. [[Economy is not about frugal behavior|True economy]] is not cheese-paring meanness but a systematic principle, and [[Tracking expenditure reveals hidden luxuries|tracking expenditure]] is the diagnostic tool that reveals where the margin actually leaks. Without this discipline, even high [[Income must exceed expenditure|income]] cannot produce lasting wealth.
