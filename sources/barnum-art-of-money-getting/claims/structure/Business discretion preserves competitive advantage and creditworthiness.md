What a business conceals matters as much as what it advertises. [[Secrecy maintains business advantage|Revealing business secrets]] to neighbors gains nothing and invites competition, while [[Reputation loss precedes financial loss|publicly announcing losses]] destroys reputation faster than the losses themselves destroy [[Capital]]. Discretion extends to financial obligations: [[Endorsements without security enable ruin|endorsing notes without security]] enables a friend's borrowing habit that escalates into [[Speculation]] and mutual ruin. In each case, the error is the sameexposing information or liability that should remain controlled.
