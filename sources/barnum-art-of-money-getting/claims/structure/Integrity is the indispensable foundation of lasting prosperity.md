Barnum's final and most emphatic principle is that [[Integrity is more valuable than wealth|integrity surpasses diamonds and rubies]] as the foundation of success. The mechanism is both negative and positive: [[Dishonesty inevitably discovered destroys opportunity|dishonesty is always eventually discovered]], closing nearly every path to future success, while [[Honesty enables community credit access|known honesty]] opens the purses of an entire community to even a poor person. This framework elevates [[Wealth]]-getting from mere acquisition to moral enterprise: [[Wealth pursuit requires accepting responsibility|the desire for wealth is laudable]] only when the pursuer accepts responsibility to enlarge human happiness. At the broadest scale, [[Commerce is civilization's foundation|commerce parallels civilization itself]], wherever [[Commerce]] flourishes, art and science produce their noblest fruits.
