Barnum argues that [[Tobacco]] is naturally abhorrent to humans (no creature except worms love it), yet people deliberately cultivate addiction. The habit becomes stronger than natural appetites; he confesses to smoking ten to fifteen cigars daily until quitting fourteen years prior, and notes the appetite intensifies the more one indulges.
