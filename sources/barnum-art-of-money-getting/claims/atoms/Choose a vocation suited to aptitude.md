Barnum argues that natural diversity in talents means different people have innate aptitudes; forcing someone into an unsuitable [[Vocation]] creates lifelong struggle. A natural mechanic thrives while an unmechanical person forced into watchmaking will succeed technically but remain miserable, working "up hill" and seeking excuses to escape.
