Barnum illustrates with a gentleman's story: his wife's elegant sofa purchase ($30,000) necessitated matching chairs, sideboards, carpets, and tables; this forced them to build a new house, and the yearly maintenance costs rose to $11,000. One impulsive purchase cascaded into permanent lifestyle expansion. Sudden [[Wealth]] is a severer ordeal than adversity; sudden wealth tempts people to expand their lifestyle immediately. Pride and vanity, once indulged, become "the undying canker-worm which gnaws the very vitals of a man's worldly possessions."
