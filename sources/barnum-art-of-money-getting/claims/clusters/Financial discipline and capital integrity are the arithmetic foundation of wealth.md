Barnum's financial logic begins with a single rule: [[True economy is disciplined spending that maintains income over expenditure|income must exceed expenditure]], enforced not by penny-pinching but by systematic awareness of where money goes. The second pillar is the avoidance of [[Debt enslaves the borrower while enriching the creditor|debt]], which reverses the arithmetic by making compound interest work against the borrower. The third is the insistence that [[Earned capital builds lasting wealth while unearned capital destroys it|capital must be self-earned]], because the discipline acquired in earning is itself the education that makes keeping money possible. Together these three principles form the financial substratum beneath every other strategy Barnum recommends.
