While internal operations keep a business alive, its external reputation determines whether it thrives. [[Advertising genuine goods creates compounding customer awareness|Persistent advertising of genuine goods]] bridges the gap between product and market, but only [[Integrity]] makes the compounding work; fraudulent goods accelerate destruction. Each customer encounter either builds or depletes goodwill, and [[Customer relationships and charitable reputation drive sustained revenue|politeness, kindness, and charitable giving]] convert individual transactions into enduring patronage. Beneath all of these stands [[Integrity is the indispensable foundation of lasting prosperity|integrity itself]], which Barnum elevates above every other principle, honesty opens the credit of an entire community, while dishonesty, once discovered, closes nearly every door.
