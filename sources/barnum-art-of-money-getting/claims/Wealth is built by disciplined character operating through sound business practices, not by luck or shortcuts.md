Barnum's complete [[System]] rests on four pillars that must all be present for [[Wealth]] to endure. First, [[Financial discipline and capital integrity are the arithmetic foundation of wealth|financial discipline]], keeping expenditure below income, avoiding [[Debt]], and insisting on [[Capital]], establishes the arithmetic precondition without which no strategy can accumulate anything. Second, [[Personal capacity, vocational fit, and relentless effort are the engine of wealth creation|personal capacity]], [[Health]], vocational alignment, and concentrated [[Perseverance]], provides the human engine that converts opportunity into output. Third, [[Sound business operations require knowledge, capable people, and strategic discretion|sound operations]], the owner's firsthand oversight, skilled employees, systematic process, and disciplined secrecy, protect and multiply what effort creates. Fourth, [[Honest dealing, genuine advertising, and charitable reputation compound into lasting commercial trust|honest reputation]], genuine [[Advertising]], courteous customer treatment, [[Charity]], and uncompromising [[Integrity]], compounds trust into the durable patronage that sustains revenue across decades. Each pillar depends on the others: discipline without effort produces nothing, effort without operations wastes itself, and operations without integrity eventually collapse. The book's recurring warning is that every shortcut, debt, inherited wealth, scattered attention, [[Speculation]], fraud, undermines the very foundation it pretends to bypass.
